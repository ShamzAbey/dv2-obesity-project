Australian Fast Food & Obesity

Exploring the relationship between obesity, food consumption, and physical activity across Australia.

Introduction

Obesity is a growing public health issue in Australia, with long-term impacts on chronic disease, wellbeing, and health services. Lifestyle factors such as food consumption, physical activity, and demographic differences all contribute to obesity patterns. This visualisation explores these patterns across states, genders, and age groups using Australian government data from the ABS and AIHW.

Obesity Rates Across Australia

Obesity rates vary between Australian states and territories. A choropleth map helps identify geographic patterns quickly, while interactive tooltips allow users to inspect each state or territory in more detail.

Changes in Food Consumption Over Time

Processed, convenience, and takeaway-style food consumption patterns have changed over time. These trends may relate to rising obesity rates because food environments influence the everyday choices available to Australians.

Physical Activity and Obesity

Key Findings

Obesity is influenced by multiple lifestyle factors, including food consumption, physical activity, geography, age, and gender. The visualisations show that geographic and demographic patterns are visible in Australian public health data, and that data visualisation can communicate complex health trends in a clear and accessible way.
